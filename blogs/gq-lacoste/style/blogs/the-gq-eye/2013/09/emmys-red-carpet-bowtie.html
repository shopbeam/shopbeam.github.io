Let's Keep the Bow Tie Where It Belongs: The Red Carpet

Jian DeLeon
September 23, 2013 1:30 pm
Facebook
Twitter

Bow ties have a bad rap. It’s not their fault. Long a staple of preppy style, Southern dandies, the once-proud form of neckwear has since become the international symbol of the stylish try-hard. Maybe it’s because the neo-preppy look has gone the way of Rugby Ralph Lauren, or maybe it’s due to the appropriation of the bow tie by trend-addled miscreants who wore them with things like short-sleeved button-down shirts and obnoxiously thick glasses, but they’ve most definitely fallen out of favor with the savvy menswear masses. And then they started disappearing on the red carpet.

These days, while "black tie" may not always mean the color black, it’s virtually impossible for any guy to mess up the timeless tudo: Black suit, white shirt, black, solid satin bow tie. It’s a foolproof formula for looking snazzy. The bow tie will never look out of place or fall from grace when it comes to any occasion that requires men step up their style considerably, like you know, award shows. Yet somehow, the necktie is gaining steam among celebrities and their stylists. While there’s nothing wrong with that—and some guys pull that look off with aplomb—it’s a look that should be the exception, not the status quo.

We’re all for being able to relate to celebrities, but when the occasion calls for looking as awesome as possible, we prefer our leading men to look like well, leading men. That means they should look next level, not on the level. They should channel the glory days of Hollywood style, when awards shows looked (and felt) more like a spectacle. Instead, we’re left with a bunch of male celebs who seem to be chasing trends, rather than setting them. Let’s keep the bow tie where it belongs. Just take a look at these 12 Emmys red carpet rigs. All of them would’ve been much better with a bow tie.

Patterned ties have no place at the Emmys. While his suit is another issue entirely, even a simple bow tie would’ve upgraded this otherwise awful outfit.

A guy like Alec Baldwin should command respect when he steps on the red carpet. Keeping it classy with a bow tie would’ve made him look more regal.

PIctured next to his Behind the Candelabra co-star, Damon’s necktie makes it clear who the leading man is.

Though the Dancing With the Stars choreographer went home with an award, an understated black satin bow tie would have made him look like a winner, too.

This kit’s 90% there. A bow tie would’ve sealed this tux in the realm of "timeless" rather than "trendy."

Again, if you’re aiming for the bare minimum when it comes to your outfit, always aim for the classics. The silver tie with this tux makes it look like something rented... which is never good.

Just say no to tacky embroidered neckwear, especially when all the attention’s supposed to be on your wife.

We can’t fault the guy for sticking to a uniform... but the whole "business/party" aesthetic would’ve looked slightly better with a white shirt and yep, black bow tie. For every two rules you have down pat, you get to break one.

Harington’s three-piece tux looks great, but the necktie takes it down a notch. Leave that look to business-types. Red carpet soirees aren’t just for dressing to the nines—they’re for turning it up to 11.

A solid satin bow tie would’ve upped the "I do whatever I want" factor that Al Pacino gives off with those boots and sunglasses... but he should still unbutton that bottom button.

NPH’s burgundy blazer netted him compliments, but it would’ve been better as the centerpiece of his kit. Dialing down everything else—like that striped necktie$mdash;would’ve been ten times more stylish.

One of Tyrion Lannister’s best outfits is his form-fitting suit of armor. A tudo should look equally tailored and impervious. Switching out the necktie for a bow tie would have achieved that.
