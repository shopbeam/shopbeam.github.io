U-S-A! U-S-A!

Last night, the American hockey squad undid Team Canada—aka The NHL Captains’ Union, aka God’s awesomest gift to the awesomest sport in the world (we’re pretty sure commentators seriously dropped the latter half of that superlative)—with a gutsy 5-3 victory that, technically, has no legitimate medal implications, but that adds about 10,000 psi of pressure to the already-slumping shoulders of the Canadians. After all, this is a team who was greeted in Vancouver with this encouraging sentiment: "If you don’t win gold, then the entirety of the Games will be considered a Failure." Fun! This on top of the fact that the US-of-A is kicking The Northern Neighbor’s ass in every other event, dominating the overall medal count, and having a pretty gosh darned good time doing it, too. Oh, Canada. —Daniel Riley

**

Betty White is going to host ’SNL’. You can stop sending us that petition now, OK? **

It turns out, 500,000 Facebook fans can’t be wrong.

That’s the number of digital signatures it took to convince Lorne Michaels to let Betty White, erstwhile Golden Girl, host Saturday Night Live. There’s a catch, however. The 88-year-old comedian—who proved her durability and ease with gross-out humor in last year’s The Proposal opposite, gulp, Oscar-nominee Sandra Bullock—will have to split hosting duties as part of a special Women in Comedy night. If rumors prove true, Betty White will be joined by SNL alums Molly Shannon, Amy Poehler and Tina Fey. It sounds like fun! But also a bit of a cop out. I mean, next week Jennifer Lopez is scheduled to do double duty as both host AND musical guest. Couldn’t she use some help?

We’re starting a new Facebook group: Invite Rachel Dratch To Co-Host With Betty White. Because, ya know, we just feel like she shouldn’t be home the night her three best friends host SNL. —Mickey Rapkin

From the Files of Art, Life, Imitation

We were fascinated by the BBC story about how there have been multiple calls from Gordon Brown’s office to England’s National Bullying Hotline because he’s such a horrible boss. Adults! Who work for the GOVERNMENT! Calling for anti-bullying tips! Life imitating In The Loop!—Alex Pappademas

The Only Book a Real Man Needs This February

For some people, the coming of Spring is demonstrated by slightly longer days, subtle shifts in the light and the fact that, you know, we’re no longer buried under an metric ton of snow. Those people are simps. Spring is REALLY marked by the release of the new Baseball Prospectus Handbook—which, as every red blooded American male knows is the essential read every February.

So we spent our weekend reading. A few choice quotes from their always-witty player write-ups:

On Paul Bako: Let this be a lesson to every parent: if your kid hits lefty, but just not that well, get him some catching gear, because the good-enough bar doesn’t get any lower than this.

On Johnny Damon His arm is still useful for lifting forks, pens, and other small items.

On Jacoby Ellsbury: Plays center field as if he were lost in a hedge maze... Jim Rice thinks Ellsbury has Hall of Fame potential, which is true if your work under the assumption that the standards will continue to erode, thanks to the induction of players like Jim Rice.

On Chris Getz: A lot of Royals fans were thrilled to get him in the Mark Teahen trade, but at this point, Royals fans are impressed whenever they see a carbon-based life form in powder-blue pants."

On Travis Ishikawa: He is someone whose threat to right-handed pitching rated up there with a girlfriend’s mom: inconvenient but unavoidable, so don’t slip up while she’s in the room.

On Brian Moehler: Surely, most of you have skipped ahead by not to start reading about Roy Oswalt. I mean, if Moehler’s on your fantasy team, how deep is your league?—James Stahl

The Incredibly Sad Search For Andrew Koening

[

The name wasn’t immediately familiar to us either. But Koening isn’t just the actor who played Boner on Growing Pains, he’s the brother-in-law and work partner of VERGE friend Jimmy Pardo, he of the Never Not Funny podcast and coverage of our Men of the Year party. Koening, as it turns out, is a talented and troubled guy, who was suffering from depression when he disappeared on Valentines Day in Vancouver and hasn’t been seen since.

This is what is great and terrible about the internet all at once. People are motivated. Thousands are searching for the guy. But everyone seems to know that something Very Bad has probably happened. A happy ending is unlikely. The tweets and Facebook updates and blog posts that slammed our in box this weekend were alternately sad and morbid, empathetic and downright stupid. (We spent Saturday convinced this was a hoax, if for no other reason than most posts were titled BONER GOES MISSING.)

It could be a prank, of course, but we doubt it. This disharmony between taste and truth is what the internet is all about these days. God willing, they find the poor guy. But would so many have tuned in if the word "boner" and the probability of a sad ending weren’t involved? Somehow, we doubt it.

Here is the missing persons site, for any of our Vancouver readers.—Dan Fierman
