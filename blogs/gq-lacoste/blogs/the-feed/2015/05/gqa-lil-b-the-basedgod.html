Lil B the BasedGod's Advice for New College Grads

Chris Gayomali
May 27, 2015 7:57 am
Facebook
Twitter

Photo: Getty Images

Lil B, who for the past half-decade has been an arbiter of spacey, discombobulated cool, sounds a little flustered. Originally I got in touch with the BasedGod in hopes of soliciting some sagely life advice for new college graduates. But with the frenzy of the NBA Western Conference Finals winding to a close, the Berkeley rapper (and Warriors fan!) has once again reared his beautiful shining face in the news cycle, and is currently on a mini media blitz, thanks, in part, to supposedly supernatural ties to Houston Rockets beard-o James Harden (who may or may not have illicitly borrowed the BasedGod’s cooking dance).

GQ managed to catch up with Lil B shortly after he ducked into a CVS to buy a charger for his phone, which was running out of battery.

Is your phone charged? Where are you at?

Right now I’m just hanging out in the Bay Area.

One of the editors here included your GQ Magazine track in a work email the other day.

I love it. GQ Magazine, strapped with the magazine! We ’bout to get it big with that first cover. Shouts out to A$AP Rocky, I seen you getting that love on that GQ. That’s a big look, man. Lil B, coming up next.

I heard you’re going to the Warriors-Rockets game on Wednesday. Do you know where you’re sitting yet?

I gotta still figure that out! I wanna be with my folks, you know what I’m saying? Somewhere close behind these players. A ticket costs enough! [Laughs]

Are you gonna be tweeting?

Definitely, if I feel it’s necessary. Other than that I just keep everything pretty authentic. Say what I feel.

Can I ask you for a small favor? Can you do like a reverse-curse on Steph Curry to make sure he doesn’t get hurt?

Yeah, you know, that curse stuff, that’s really up to the BasedGod. Me as Lil B, I do know the BasedGod. But all that, it’s really up to him. I just try to stay positive and keep it loving, you know what I mean?

Has James Harden or his camp reached out to you at all about the cooking dance stuff?

Nah, nah. The problem with them is that, they definitely do that silent treatment stuff. He learned a lot from Kevin Durant, you know?

What about the Warriors? Do they show you love?

I get a lot of support from the Warriors organization. The Oracle Arena. A lot of the Warriors follow me. Shouts out to Klay Thompson. Marreese Speights. These are just a few I can think of off the top of my head. It’s all love.

I remember a few years ago you challenged Kevin Durant to a game of one-on-one. Is there anyone on this year’s Dubs team whose game most closely resembles yours?

Right now I really don’t know. I’m working on my fundamentals. I’m a really good defensive player. I’ve been working on my defensive stance. Defensive stance, fosho! You know, just working on getting things together. Getting that consistent shot.

Are you trying to pattern your shot more after Steph or Klay?

I wish my shot was like that! Right now, I have my own unique shot. I’m really stronger going to the hole on the drive. I’m more of a defensive shooting guard. Yeah, that’s kind of the way I play.

What would you do if the Warriors won the title this year?

I’m just gonna be extremely, eternally happy. It’s gonna be great motivation to see your local team doing great things and bringing inspiration. And bringing things to new heights and levels. I’m definitely just excited about life. And life is really good right now.

I want everybody to know that they’re great. I love GQ.

Let’s change gears a bit and talk about your fans who are graduating from college right now. I think I read somewhere that you were a women’s study major. Is that true?

Sometimes these internet things... they’ll say some pretty cool stuff about me. But that’s not true. No women’s studies, but I get the people that think that way. [Laughs] Definitely interested in getting an associates degree soon and trying to continue to learn, you know what I mean? Really excited to keep learning.

What advice would you give to new college grads, the kinds of kids who maybe used Lil B quotes in their yearbooks?

Really just.... [there’s a call on the other line] damn, this is CBS calling me. What I would say to them is that just continue to stay positive, figure out their passion—what they would love to do for free without any money. See how far that can go, but really just getting together with people. Get the support of other people. People make things happen.

Do you think it’s realistic that everyone should figure out what they’re passionate about, and pursue that?

Yeah, definitely. [And here, he sounds distracted.] Definitely.

If you weren’t a rapper/author/internet human, what do you think you would be doing career-wise?

I’m not too sure. Maybe neurosurgeon. Something in the health field. Biology, dealing with the earth. The animals. It would most likely be some kind of doctor. Neuroscience, neurosurgeon. I always wanted to do that, ever since I was young. Before rap!

But it’s never too late to go back and study that. [I could still] be that neurosurgeon that I really wanna be. I’d probably be the first to do it if I just stick to it.

So some of these kids who spent their teens listening to you...

Hey bruh. Brother, can you actually hold on real fast? On the other line is CBS and they keep blowing me up. It’s a live radio show. Can you hold on one second?

Oh, sure.

[Holds for not even 3 minutes]

Hey bruh! Sorry about that. That was a live radio show! Quickest possible thing. Now we back to it! Now I can give you that focus!

So these new grads! What advice would you give them for the job hunt?

First they gotta realize how beautiful they are. They are amazing. They are survivors because they are alive.

That means they’re special regardless of any of the pain, or any of the second guessing we go through as humans. The first thing is realize you’re beautiful, inside out. And we’re not just talking about looks. We’re talking about personality, thinking—just being a great overall person. Know you’re a great person.

The second thing is, know that every day will not be the same forever. There might be something that’s going on that’s weird right now. But you’ll be finding your job soon. You’ll be finding what you need to find out about real soon.

Let the earth send you blessings, you know? Get support from people. Build your community. Build your friends. Get some solid friends. Believe in each other. Try something new. Because that will go to the next step. Everything is always about going to the next step. And you don’t have to step on people’s toes to get there.

It doesn’t have to be about capital. It doesn’t have to be about you making money to get to the next place. Things can be done out of love.

And you can make a living off love.

I hear a lot of people who say, "Love doesn’t pay the bills." Yes they do. It pays mine!

Off the top of your head, what Lil B tracks would you recommend to someone who might be bummed by the job hunt?

We got um, okay. You could play Lil B "Foolin Around." That’s one.

Let’s do Lil B... play that "Rain in England" produced by the BasedGod, ecutive produced by Lil B, produced and composed by the BasedGod. Listen to that thoroughly and in full.

And put on "Choices and Flowers" by the BaseGod, produced and composed by the BasedGod, ecutive produced by Lil B.

There’s no rapping on that one. Check that out. Get your mind right. It’s a beautiful life.
