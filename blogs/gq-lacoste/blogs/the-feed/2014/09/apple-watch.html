The Stylish Man's Guide to the New Apple Watch

Daniel Dumas
September 9, 2014 3:45 pm
Facebook
Twitter

Photos: The Apple Watch’s First Fashion Shoot

If you exist on planet Earth, you might have heard that Apple unleashed a glut of fresh products to a pulsating morass of salivating nerds. Bigger, faster iPhones! Mobile payment systems! But CEO Tim Cook also unveiled an all-new product that sits squarely in the fashion realm: the Apple Watch. And here’s what you need to know:

OK. What’s the deal here?

The Apple Watch is essentially a small touchscreen attached to a strap. It comes in two sizes (38 mm or 42 mm wide) and three different trims: a base version, a sporty model geared towards athletes, and a fancy edition made from 18-karat gold. All three are capable of doing the same things—and there are many of those things. The Apple Watch is fitness tracker that can record the steps you’ve taken during the day and your heart rate. It can give you directions, display email, and let you use voice commands to respond to texts. You’ll be able to wave your wrist in places like Whole Foods to pay a bill. It’s also capable of high-level functions such as sending your pals teensy sketches, messages, and emojis. Since the screen’s only an inch-and-a-half tall, there’s a nifty little knob on the side—the crown, in watch parlance—that helps you scroll, select, and feel less like a sausage-fingered oaf in need of a dialing wand.

So...does it feel like a regular watch?

In a word: hell no. Apple worked hard on the design of the, um, Apple Watch. It’s lightweight and you can swap in different straps really easily, from sweat resistant sporty numbers to an of-the-moment Milanese mesh bracelet. Most look great, for good reason: Apple hired legit fashion experts like the former CEO of Yves St. Lauren and an ecutive from Burberry to work on the watch.

Does this mean I can pawn my Rolex Submariner?

You should absolutely NOT do that. Apple would love it if you never wore another watch again, but we can’t quite endorse that idea. The Apple Watch certainly has a lot going for it from a technological standpoint, but we’ll never vouch for wearing a digital watch with a tux. If you have a special occasion coming up, opt for something classic (read: analog). It could be anything from a Rolex Oyster to a Shinola Runwell to a Skagen Ancher. Remember; the Apple Watch is built to check emails. You don’t want your wrist lighting up with a message from the CEO when you’re toasting the groom. Or delivering a eulogy. Or skinny dipping in a moonlit Caribbean bay with a cute girl you met at a local tiki bar. Think of it this way: the Apple Watch won’t replace your snazziest timepiece, only because it doesn’t have to. You can own more than one watch, according to U.S. law.*

Real talk: will this thing change the way people interact with their gadgets?

We’re about to turn the corner into an age where people will spend a lot of time nervously fiddling with their watches instead of fiddling with their phones. You won’t have the physical barrier of pulling it out of your pocket to fight the urge—that screen will always be there, calling out, in the corner of your eye. Our advice? Start steeling yourself now against the magnetism of an omnipresent screen literally attached to your body. Smartwatches like the Apple Watch will earn their keep by delivering us the info we need, when we need it, without us asking for it. We just gotta stay strong in the face of wrist-mounted emoji.

I want one now. Give me one!

Sorry man, you’ll have to wait until spring 2015 for the pleasure of paying at least $350 for the Apple Watch.

Should I wait in line on opening day to get one?

Under no circumstances should you do that.

*We are not lawyers. But we’re pretty sure.
