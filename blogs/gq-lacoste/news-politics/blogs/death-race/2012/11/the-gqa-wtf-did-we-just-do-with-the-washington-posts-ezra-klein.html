WTF Did We Just Do, Ezra Klein?

Marin Cogan
November 8, 2012 6:57 am
Facebook
Twitter

Still trying to wrap your head around that 18-month, six-billion dollar campaign? Not quite sure what to make of Tuesday’s results? Not to worry: for the next few days we’ll be surveying the best and brightest in politics and punditry to help you make sense of it all.

With Ezra Klein, editor of Wonkblog at The Washington Post, columnist for Bloomberg View, MSNBC contributor.

GQ: What the f*ck did we just do for the last year?

EK: We had an election! I’m not on-board with the whiners who keep saying this election wasn’t about big enough ideas or was too dirty. There was a serious, raucous argument over tax reform, Medicare, tax increases vs. investments, the pace of deficit reduction, and more. I should say, for the record, that I don’t really believe in election mandates, so I don’t necessarily think this election answered these questions. But this was, by and large, what an election is supposed to be: There were two different visions put forward, argued over, and then the American people made a choice.

GQ: What does Republican party need to do now?

EK: Change. The GOP has lost the popular vote in five of the last six presidential elections. I don’t think the situation has felt that dire to them because they tend to do better in midterms and because SCOTUS handed them the win, and the presidency, in 2000. But for comparison, when Bill Clinton and the DLC were reforming the Democratic Party in the 1990s, the Democratic Party, which by that point admitted it was in trouble, had lost the popular vote in...five of the last six presidential election (68, 72, 80, 84, 88).

GQ: What does the president need to do in his second term?

EK: His most lasting accomplishments, I think, will come from consolidating the achievements of his first term. He’ll need to get the Affordable Care Act up and running. Get Dodd-Frank in place. Use the leverage he’s got on tas to get some kind of balanced fiscal deal. What’s left, then, is trying to use the GOP’s demographic disadvantage as leverage to get something done on immigration, and figuring out something on climate change. I’d like to say I’m more optimistic on that last bit than I am.

GQ: What’s the lesson you’re taking away from this election?

EK: The polls, when aggregated properly, are usually right. Pundits, particularly when they’re trying to convince themselves that the trailing candidate can win, are usually wrong.

GQ: What is the most astonishing thing about the race to you?

EK: How purely it fit the fundamentals-based predictions. Wonkblog worked with three political scientists in April to develop an election prediction model that used data we had by June. The point was to show how predictable elections were six months before the vote. At this moment, it looks like our model, remarkably, called the popular vote within a tenth of a percentage point.

GQ: Give us one song that sums up your feelings about the end of the campaign.

EK: This doesn’t sum up my feelings about the campaign so much as it sums up my feelings about being done covering the campaign, but "Shots," by LMFAO.
